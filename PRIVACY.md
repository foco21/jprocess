# Privacy Policy — JuneProcess

**Last updated: June 17, 2026**

JuneProcess ("the app") is a free, open-source camera application. This policy explains how the
app handles your information. In short: **the app does not collect, store, or transmit any
personal data.**

## Data collection

JuneProcess does **not** collect, store, share, or transmit any personal or usage data.

The app requests **no internet permission** — it does not include the Android permission required
to send data off your device, so it is technically incapable of transmitting your information
anywhere. There are no analytics, no advertising, no crash-reporting services, no tracking, and
no third-party SDKs that collect data.

## Photos you take

Photos and RAW (`.dng`) files you capture are saved **only to your device's local storage**
(your gallery / DCIM folder). They are never uploaded, never sent to the developer, and never
shared with any third party. You retain full control of them through your device at all times.

## Permissions and why they are used

- **Camera** — used solely to show the viewfinder and capture photos on your device. This is
  required for the app to function.
- **Photos and media** (`READ_MEDIA_IMAGES`, `ACCESS_MEDIA_LOCATION`, and legacy storage
  permissions on older Android versions) — used solely to save your captured photos to your
  gallery and to display a thumbnail of your most recent photo inside the app. These are
  optional; declining them does not prevent you from taking or saving photos on modern Android.

All image processing happens entirely on your device.

## Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children, because it does not
collect any data at all.

## Third parties

The app does not share data with any third parties, because it does not collect any.
